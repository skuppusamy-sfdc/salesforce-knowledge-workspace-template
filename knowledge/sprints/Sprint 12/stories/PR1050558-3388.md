## Description

As a System Administrator, when managing client contracts, I want to relate a Practice Operations contract, a Healthcare Practitioner Facility, and a specific Process Type, so that I have a tracking mechanism of the type of process needed to update the clinician’s contract based on the changes made at the client contract level.

## Solution

Created by Gemini

### ## Part 1: Analyze Contract Changes

This first part kicks off automatically when a contract's status is set to "Executed." Its job is to analyze all the detailed changes in the contract and sort them into organized groups.

1. **Gather Data:** It starts by collecting every single payment agreement change associated with that contract. It also makes a special note of any "mixed bag" facilities where pay rates for the same shift are inconsistent, as these require manual review.

1. **Classify Changes:** It then looks at each change onebyone and applies a set of rules to categorize it. Each change is tagged with a reason (e.g., "Stipend Change," "Rate Decreased") and assigned a process type based on a strict hierarchy:

#** **Manual:* For complex or high-risk changes.

#** **Agent:* For simple, routine changes that still need a person to review them.

#** **Automation:* For very straightforward changes that the system can handle on its own.

1. **Create Summaries:** Instead of dealing with hundreds of small changes, it creates a handful of summary records. Each summary record represents one facility and contains the final process type (Manual, Agent, or Automation) and a list of all the reasons for the change.

1. **Launch Part 2:** Once it's finished, it automatically triggers the second part of the process, handing off the contract ID.

### ## Part 2: Create Actionable Records

This second job takes the organized summaries from Part 1 and creates the final records that people will work with.

1. **Get Summaries and People:** It fetches the summary records created in Part 1. Then, it finds every active healthcare professional who works at the affected facilities under that contract.

1. **Create Final Records:** For each of those healthcare professionals, it creates a new "Client Change Management" record. This record links the professional to the contract and assigns the appropriate **Process Type** (Manual, Agent, or Automation) that was determined in Part 1.

Essentially, the entire process takes a complex and messy set of contract changes and turns them into a clean, organized list of tasks, each assigned to the correct processing channel.
