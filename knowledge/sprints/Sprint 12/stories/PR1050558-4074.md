## Description

**Detailed Description:** Provider Contracting members, specifically the specialists, need to be able to view CTI records from the Person record, and we need to be able to see whether the clinician is contracting individually or through a business entity, and the name of said entity from the Contract and Agreement records. We have no way of verifying or identifying it otherwise.

for the Contract/Agreement records, if they are contracting individually, then the record should have a field that says Individual and a field that shows what the signature block entry will look like (John Doe, MD)

If they are contracting through an entity, then the record should have a field that says Entity, and a field that shows what the signature block entry will show, (ABC Corp, LLC), clinician's name and title of Authorized Signatory should pull into the Signature block, but does not need to be shown on the Contract/Agreement records

**Persona:** Clinician Contracting Specialist

**Tester:** Matthew Hebert

**Steps to Reproduce:**

Navigate to the Clinician - Approved and Term Sheet Exectd list view. Select the Contract record for Brad Allen Blaker. You will not be able to tell that he has an entity.

## Root Cause

The defect preventing the viewing of CTI records and critical contract details (e.g., individual vs. entity contracting) was caused by a **missing or incomplete configuration of user permissions**.

** **Configuration Error:* The intended users lacked the required access rights to view the CTI records and associated contract fields. The necessary permissions were not bundled or assigned to the users' profiles/permission sets during previous deployments.

## Root Cause Type

Requirements: Incomplete Requirements
