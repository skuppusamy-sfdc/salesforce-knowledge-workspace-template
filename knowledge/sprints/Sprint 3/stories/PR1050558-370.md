## Description

**As a** Salesforce Administrator and Developer, **I want to** have a standardized, generic error logging mechanism in Salesforce, **so that** all unhandled application errors, integration failures, and critical system events are consistently captured, easily reviewed, and proactively addressed to maintain system health and data integrity

## Acceptance Criteria

** **Error Object Definition:*

** A custom object, `Error_Log__c`, is created to store error details.

** The `Error_Log__c` object includes fields to capture key information, such as:

*** `Error_Message__c` (Long Text Area)

*** `Error_Type__c` (Picklist, e.g., Apex, Integration, Validation, UI)

*** `Source_Component__c` (Text, e.g., Apex Class/Method, LWC, Flow, OmniScript Name, Integration Procedure Name)

*** `Record_Id__c` (Lookup/Text, if related to a specific record)

*** `User_Id__c` (Lookup to User, capturing who triggered the error)

*** `Error_Timestamp__c` (DateTime, auto-populated upon creation)

*** `Status__c` (Picklist, e.g., New, Investigating, Resolved, Duplicate)

*** `Severity__c` (Picklist, e.g., Critical, High, Medium, Low)

*** `Context_JSON__c` (Long Text Area, for additional relevant data at time of error, e.g., input parameters, partial payload)

*** `Stack_Trace__c` (Long Text Area, for Apex errors)

*** `Is_Handled__c` (Checkbox, true if error was caught and logged gracefully)

*** Access for **Corporate Support Group* as per Security Matrix

*** Access for **System Administrator* as per Security Matrix

** **Logging Mechanism (Apex Utility Class):*

** A global Apex utility class (`ErrorLogger` or similar) is created to facilitate logging.

** The utility class includes a static method (e.g., `logError(errorMessage, errorType, sourceComponent, ...)` that allows developers to easily log errors with required and optional parameters.

** The logging method handles DML operations for creating `Error_Log__c` records.

** The logging method uses `try-catch` internally to prevent the logging process itself from causing further unhandled exceptions.

** The logging method can accept a `JSON` string for the `Context_JSON__c` field.
